What streaming APIs solve

Streaming APIs deliver event sequences over time, unlike one-shot request-response APIs. They are ideal for analytics, telemetry, and live dashboards.

  • Producer emits events
  • Broker/transport routes events
  • Consumer processes events with checkpoints

Delivery semantics

  • At-most-once: fastest, potential loss
  • At-least-once: safer, duplicates possible
  • Exactly-once: expensive and context-dependent

In practice, most teams use at-least-once + idempotent consumers.

Checkpointing example

{
  "consumer": "billing-service",
  "partition": 3,
  "offset": 928411,
  "updatedAt": "2026-05-21T09:12:00Z"
}

Checkpoint persistence is what lets consumers resume safely after restart.
